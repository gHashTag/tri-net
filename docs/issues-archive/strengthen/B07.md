## Goal
Add a pure-software (`-sim`) PHY modem model to `trios-mesh` that produces BER-vs-SNR curves, AMC SNR-switching thresholds, and a 5.8 GHz link-budget check — numerically comparing OFDM vs DFT-spread single-carrier — so all downstream Zynq PL RTL is de-risked before a single LUT is committed.

## Why / science
Before we spend the tight PL budget of the Mini's `xc7z020` (~85K logic cells, ~220 DSP) on an OFDM demodulator (report v2.2 §4, our top greenfield tech risk), we should settle the numbers in software. A host DSP model can quantify BER-vs-SNR for BPSK / QPSK / 16-QAM / 64-QAM under convolutional (and optional LDPC) FEC over an AWGN + mild-Ricean channel, derive the AMC SNR thresholds we switch MCS at, fix FFT size / cyclic-prefix length / the MCS table, and validate the 5.8 GHz link budget including the 4–7 dB OFDM PAPR back-off penalty versus low-PAPR DFT-spread SC. This is the same "testable like the crypto core" discipline that got M1 green: pure functions, deterministic seeds, asserted thresholds — no hardware.

The open-sky drone channel is close to AWGN with a mild Ricean LOS component, which is exactly the regime where SC vs OFDM trade PAPR back-off (hurting our weak 10–15 dBm Mini PA) against multipath robustness. Getting the link budget right here directly attacks two HIGH weak spots: weak Mini PA → short range, and the OFDM-fit PL risk.

Citations (real, verified):
- wirelesspi.com — "Why OFDM is used in UAV / drone communication links" (PAPR, CP, multipath rationale; informal DSP tutorial).
- ETRI Journal, doi:10.4218/etrij.15.0114.1194 — Xia & Ji, "Reducing PAPR of SC-FDMA Signals through Simple Amplitude Predistortion," 2015;37(5):922–928 (SC-FDMA = DFT-spread OFDM low-PAPR uplink; motivates the SC-vs-OFDM PAPR back-off comparison).
- NASA NTRS 20170001299 — "Adaptive Coding and Modulation Experiment With NASA's Space Communication and Navigation (SCaN) Testbed" (real-link ACM / MCS-switching over DVB-S2, directly relevant to the AMC threshold table we derive here).
